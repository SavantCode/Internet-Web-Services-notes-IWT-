An **Architectural Page Mockup** is a **visual blueprint or schematic of a website page** that shows the **layout, structure, and placement of content and elements** before actual development begins.

It is **not the final design**, but a planning tool to **organize content, navigation, and functionality** so developers and designers know what goes where.

---

## **A. Purpose of Architectural Page Mockups**

1. **Visual Planning:** Helps visualize how content and elements will appear.
2. **Content Organization:** Shows which information goes where.
3. **Navigation Layout:** Helps design menus, buttons, links, and user flow.
4. **Feedback Tool:** Allows clients and team members to review structure before coding.
5. **Time & Cost Saving:** Reduces redesigns by resolving structural issues early.

---

## **B. Components of a Page Mockup**

A typical mockup includes:

* **Header:** Logo, site name, navigation bar.
* **Main Content Area:** Text, images, videos, articles.
* **Sidebar (if any):** Menus, advertisements, links, widgets.
* **Footer:** Contact info, social media links, copyright.
* **Interactive Elements:** Buttons, forms, search bar.

---

## **C. Types of Architectural Page Mockups**

1. **Low-Fidelity Mockups (Wireframes):**

   * Simple sketches or black-and-white outlines.
   * Focus on **structure and placement**, not colors or styles.
   * Quick and easy to create.
   *    * *Example:* Hand-drawn layout showing header, content, sidebar, and footer boxes.


2. **High-Fidelity Mockups:**

   * Detailed designs with colors, fonts, and images.
   * Close to the **final visual appearance** of the page.
   *  Useful for client presentations.
   *    * *Example:* Photoshop or Figma design that looks almost like the final website.


---

## **D. Benefits**

* Improves communication between designers, developers, and clients.
* Identifies design and usability issues early.
* Ensures content hierarchy is logical.
* Makes website development more efficient.

Architectural Page Mockups are **visual blueprints of website pages** that show the placement of content, navigation, and interactive elements before development.

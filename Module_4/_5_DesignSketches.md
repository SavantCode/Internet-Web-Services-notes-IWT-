**Definition:**
Design sketches are **rough, hand-drawn or digital drawings of a website‚Äôs layout and structure**. They are created **before detailed mockups or actual development**, to **visualize ideas quickly and communicate them** to the team or client.

In simple words:

> ‚ÄúDesign sketches are the first rough drafts of a website page showing where content, images, menus, and buttons will go.‚Äù

---

## **A. Purpose of Design Sketches**

1. **Idea Visualization:** Quickly translate thoughts into visual format.

   * *Example:* Sketching a homepage layout with a logo at the top, content in the middle, and links on the side.
2. **Planning Content Placement:** Helps decide where text, images, videos, and menus should appear.
3. **Communication Tool:** Designers, developers, and clients can **discuss layout ideas** before investing time in high-fidelity mockups.
4. **Exploring Multiple Options:** Easy to create **different layouts** and choose the best one.
5. **Time and Cost Saving:** Fixing design issues at the sketch stage is faster and cheaper than later.

---

## **B. Characteristics of Design Sketches**

* Quick and simple, not detailed.
* Focus on **layout and structure**, not colors or fonts.
* Can be **hand-drawn or digital** (using tools like Adobe XD, Figma, or just paper and pencil).
* Often includes **labels for content areas**, buttons, menus, and images.

---

## **C. Components Usually Shown in Design Sketches**

1. **Header:** Logo, navigation menu, search bar.
2. **Main Content Area:** Articles, text, images, or product information.
3. **Sidebar (optional):** Links, ads, or widgets.
4. **Footer:** Contact info, social media links, copyright.
5. **Interactive Elements:** Buttons, forms, call-to-action areas.

---

## **D. Difference Between Design Sketches and Architectural Mockups**

| Feature                 | Design Sketch                | Architectural Mockup                     |
| ----------------------- | ---------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Level of Detail         | Low (rough layout)           | Medium/High (placement + style)          |
| Purpose                 | Brainstorming & planning     | Detailed blueprint for development       |
| Appearance              | Hand-drawn or simple digital | Digital, can include colors/fonts/images |
| Stage in Design Process | Early stage                  | After sketches, before coding            |

## **F. Advantages**

1. Fast and easy to create.
2. Allows multiple design ideas to be tested quickly.
3. Encourages team collaboration.
4. Reduces errors and redesigns at later stages.
5. Serves as the **foundation for architectural mockups and high-fidelity designs**.

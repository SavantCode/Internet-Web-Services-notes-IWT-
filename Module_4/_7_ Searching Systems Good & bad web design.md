# **Searching Systems & Web Design: Good vs Bad**

### **1. Searching Systems in Websites**

**Definition:**
A searching system allows users to **find specific content or pages** within a website using keywords.

**Key Points:**

* Common on large websites (e-commerce, news portals, universities).
* Improves **usability** and **user satisfaction**.
* Can include **search bars, filters, and advanced search options**.

**Example:**

* Amazon’s search bar with filters for categories, price, ratings.
* University site search: “Courses → Computer Science → Admission Form.”

---

### **2. Good Web Design**

**Characteristics:**

1. **Clear Layout:** Organized content, readable fonts, consistent colors.
2. **Easy Navigation:** Intuitive menus, breadcrumbs, and search.
3. **Fast Loading:** Pages open quickly.
4. **Responsive Design:** Works on all devices.
5. **Accessibility:** Usable by all, including disabled users.

**Example:**

* Amazon, Apple, and BBC websites.

---

### **3. Bad Web Design**

**Characteristics:**

1. **Cluttered Pages:** Too much text or images.
2. **Confusing Navigation:** Menus hard to find or inconsistent.
3. **Slow Loading:** Pages take too long to open.
4. **Non-Responsive:** Not mobile-friendly.
5. **Poor Search:** Search bar missing or returns irrelevant results.

**Example:**

* Websites with 50+ menu items, broken links, or no search functionality.
